# Government Tender Compliance Research
## BidForge AI — Industry Standards & Compliance Reference

### 1. Government Tender Types (Common Framework)

| Tender Type | Acronym | Description | Typical Value |
|---|---|---|---|
| Request for Tender | RFT | Formal, structured procurement for goods/services | >$50K |
| Request for Proposal | RFP | Solution-based procurement; evaluative criteria | >$100K |
| Request for Quotation | RFQ | Low-value, simple procurement | <$50K |
| Expression of Interest | EOI | Pre-qualification stage before RFT/RFP | N/A |
| Panel Arrangement | PANEL | Pre-approved supplier list for ongoing work | Ongoing |
| Sole Source / Direct Sourcing | — | Single supplier, limited competition | Varies |

### 2. Standard Tender Document Structure (Government)

Most government RFx documents follow this structure:

```
1. COVER / TITLE PAGE
   - Tender ID, title, closing date/time
   - Respondent legal name & ABN/ACN
   - Authorised signatory & contact

2. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (2-3 pages)
   - Understanding of the requirement
   - Proposed solution overview
   - Key differentiators
   - Estimated value

3. COMPANY CAPABILITY (5-10 pages)
   - Company overview, history, structure
   - Relevant licences, accreditations, certifications
   - Financial viability statement
   - Insurance coverage details
   - Quality management system (ISO 9001, etc.)
   - Safety record (LTIFR, TRIFR stats)
   - Environmental/sustainability credentials
   - Indigenous/community engagement commitments
   - Modern slavery statement

4. TECHNICAL APPROACH / METHODOLOGY (10-20 pages)
   - Detailed understanding of scope
   - Proposed methodology/workflow
   - Timeline / schedule
   - Resource allocation plan
   - Risk management approach
   - Innovation / value-adds
   - Transition / mobilisation plan
   - Performance KPIs & reporting

5. KEY PERSONNEL (3-5 pages)
   - Organisational chart for contract
   - Key role descriptions
   - CVs for nominated staff (2 pages max each)
   - Backup/succession plan

6. PAST PERFORMANCE / CASE STUDIES (5-10 pages)
   - 3-5 relevant projects/contracts
   - Client reference contacts
   - Lessons learned / continuous improvement

7. PRICING (As required)
   - Schedule of rates / fixed price breakdown
   - Volume discounts
   - Price escalation formula
   - Payment terms

8. COMPLIANCE SCHEDULE (Mandatory)
   - Response to each condition of contract
   - "Ticked box" compliance matrix
   - Exceptions / qualifications clearly marked

9. APPENDICES
   - Company policies (WHS, QA, Environment, Code of Conduct)
   - Financial statements
   - Licences & certifications
   - Insurance certificates
```

### 3. Key Compliance Mandatories

#### 3.1 Mandatory Documentation
- ✅ Signed tender form/declaration
- ✅ Certificate of Insurance (Public Liability: $20M minimum; Professional Indemnity vary)
- ✅ Workers' Compensation certificate
- ✅ WHS Policy & Safety Management Plan
- ✅ Quality Assurance certification (ISO 9001 or equivalent) *or path to certification*
- ✅ Environmental Management Plan
- ✅ Equal Opportunity / Diversity Policy
- ✅ Modern Slavery Statement
- ✅ Privacy / Data Protection compliance
- ✅ Conflict of Interest declaration
- ✅ Financial statements (last 2-3 years)
- ✅ Licences (Contractor licence, specific trade licences)

#### 3.2 Mandatory Criteria Checklist (Non-Price)
| Criterion | Typical Weight | Evidence Required |
|---|---|---|
| Capability & Experience | 30-40% | Case studies, CVs, refs |
| Methodology & Approach | 20-30% | Work plan, risk mgmt |
| Quality & Compliance | 10-15% | Certifications, audits |
| Safety & WHS | 10-15% | WHS system, stats |
| Sustainability & ESG | 5-10% | Green credentials, social value |
| Price | 20-30% | Pricing schedule |

#### 3.3 Common Disqualifiers
- ❌ Documents not submitted by deadline
- ❌ Missing mandatory declarations
- ❌ Inadequate insurance levels
- ❌ Insufficient financial capacity
- ❌ Unlicensed/unregistered operator
- ❌ Unresolved conflict of interest
- ❌ Failure to address ALL scope items
- ❌ Non-compliant pricing format

### 4. Industry-Specific Requirements

#### 4.1 Logistics & Transport
- Heavy Vehicle National Law (HVNL) compliance
- Chain of Responsibility (CoR) obligations
- Dangerous Goods licence (if applicable)
- Fatigue Management accreditation
- NHVAS accreditation (Mass, Maintenance, Fatigue)
- Fleet maintenance & inspection records
- Route management & tracking systems
- Cold Chain certification (if refrigerated)

#### 4.2 Construction
- QBCC / relevant state builder licence
- OH&S Act compliance (Site Safety Plan)
- SWMS (Safe Work Method Statements)
- Construction Induction (White Card)
- Project bank account compliance
- Security of Payment Act knowledge
- Building Code of Australia compliance
- Environmental Protection licence (demolition/civil)

#### 4.3 Security Services
- Security Providers Act compliance (state-based)
- Crowd Controller / Security Officer licences (individual)
- Monitoring Centre certification (ASIAL grade)
- Fire Safety Adviser qualifications
- CCTV & access control systems compliance
- Data privacy & surveillance laws
- Use of force policies & training
- Psychometric screening for staff

#### 4.4 Cleaning Services
- Cleaning Industry Award compliance
- Chemical use & storage regulations
- Infection control & hygiene standards
- Working with Children Check (if schools)
- NDIS Screening Check (if disability settings)
- Waste management compliance
- Equipment safety & maintenance logs
- Environmental cleaning certification (Green cleaning)

### 5. Scoring Methodology (Typical Government)

| Score | Rating | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Excellent | Exceeds requirements, significant added value |
| 4 | Good | Fully meets, some added value |
| 3 | Satisfactory | Meets all requirements |
| 2 | Marginal | Partially meets, gaps exist |
| 1 | Poor | Does not meet |

- Non-Price weighting typically 60-80% of total score
- Price weighting typically 20-40%
- Price is scored as: (Lowest Price / Tenderer's Price) × Price Weight × 100

### 6. Recommended Response Strategies

1. **Follow the response structure exactly** — evaluators work from a check-list. Omitted sections lose points.
2. **Use the compliance matrix** — Clearly respond "Compliant" or note "Alternative Proposed" with explanation.
3. **Quantify everything** — Use metrics: "Reduced incident rate by 40%", "Delivered 95% on-time".
4. **Mirror the evaluation criteria** — Address each criterion explicitly in your response.
5. **Executive summary sells** — Busy evaluators often only read the summary. Make it strong.
6. **Provide proof, not promises** — Certificates, stats, case studies, references.
7. **Tailor to the specific contract** — Generic responses are obvious and score poorly.
8. **Address risk proactively** — Identify risks and show mitigation strategies.
9. **Format professionally** — Headers, TOC, page numbers, consistent fonts, no typos.
10. **Check for probity** — No contact with evaluators outside process; all communications via portal.
